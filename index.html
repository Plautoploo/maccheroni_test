Our Pasta Varieties

Each shape tells a unique story

Elbow Macaroni

7-10 mins Al Dente

Short, curved tubes that perfectly capture creamy sauces. Originally created in northern Italy, this versatile pasta has become a global comfort food staple.

Ziti

10-12 mins Firm

Long, smooth tubes with clean-cut ends. Traditional to Campania, these elegant pipes are perfect for baked dishes and hearty meat sauces.

Penne

11-13 mins Al Dente

Cylinder-shaped with angled ends, named for their quill-like appearance. The ridged surface and hollow center make it perfect for capturing both thick and light sauces.

Our Heritage

A tradition passed through generations

The art of pasta making dates back to ancient civilizations, with the earliest known pasta being made by the Etruscans. By the 13th century, pasta had become a staple of Italian cuisine, with each region developing its unique shapes and preparations.

In Naples, the craft of pasta making reached its zenith in the 18th century, where the legendary "maccheronari" would sell fresh pasta on the streets. This tradition of artisanal pasta making continues today, with each shape and texture designed to complement specific sauces and dishes.
